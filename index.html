  • Germany (EUR): Base salaries from Eightfold AI talent dataset (50 real profiles). Cross-checked with Vergütungsradar 2025 and Glassdoor DE. Total comp calculated using employer social security (~19.5%) and typical bonus rates.

  • RSU / Stock data: levels.fyi (engineering compensation database), Glassdoor equity disclosures, and public filings for companies operating in both markets.
  • Note: EUR figures shown at EUR/USD ~1.08 (ECB 2025 avg) for context only — shown in native currency throughout. RSU values assume 4-year vesting, shown as annual equivalent.

RSU / Stock Awards — Detail View levels.fyi · Glassdoor · Public filings

What RSU means: RSU = Restricted Stock Unit. Companies give employees shares of stock that vest (unlock) over time — typically over 4 years. The numbers below show the annual equivalent value of a typical new-hire grant. In Germany, RSUs are less common; many companies use profit-sharing or phantom stock instead. In NJ, RSUs are standard at all tech/photonics companies.
🇩🇪 Germany — Typical Annual RSU / Equity by Level
German companies (ZEISS, Infineon, Nokia DE) are beginning to offer RSUs to compete with US firms, but grants are generally 40–60% smaller than NJ equivalents. Most German photonics SMEs (TOPTICA, incoatec) offer no equity at all — a potential Ciena differentiator if equity is part of the offer.
🇺🇸 New Jersey — Typical Annual RSU / Equity by Level
NJ equity is significant at the principal level and above. Nokia Bell Labs, Coherent Corp, and Thorlabs all offer competitive RSU packages. For senior NJ hires, equity is often 20–35% of total comp — Ciena must offer comparable equity to compete.
